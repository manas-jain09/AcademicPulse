# Academic Pulse: Advanced AI Educational Ecosystem

Academic Pulse is a cutting-edge, agentic educational platform specifically engineered for **CBSE Class 10 students (2025-26)**. It transcends traditional e-learning by integrating a sophisticated multi-agent architecture, a precision RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) pipeline, and multimodal AI workflows to provide a personalized, immersive, and high-fidelity study experience.

---

## 1. Multi-Agent Orchestration Architecture
The platform's intelligence is governed by an **Orchestrator-Worker** multi-agent system (`mentorAgents.ts`), which mimics a team of specialized educators.

### **The Orchestrator**
Before any response is generated, a high-level Orchestrator agent performs **Intent Classification** on the user's query. Using zero-shot JSON classification, it routes the request to one or more specialized agents:
- **Learn Intent**: Routes to the **Mentor Agent**.
- **Solve Intent**: Routes to the **Mentor Agent** for step-by-step guidance.
- **Evaluate Intent**: Routes to the **Evaluator Agent**.
- **Plan Intent**: Routes to the **Planner Agent**.

### **The Specialized Agents**
*   **Mentor Agent**: Focuses on **Socratic tutoring** and conceptual abstraction. It is constrained by strict pedagogical rules—avoiding "text-walls" and using siblings-like, encouraging language. It intelligently handles emotional support for stressed students.
*   **Evaluator Agent**: Acts as a strict but constructive CBSE examiner. It analyzes student answers against marking schemes, providing granular feedback, marks (X/Y), and "Perfect Version" examples for learning.
*   **Planner Agent**: An organizational specialist that employs **Iterative Querying**. It refuses to create a study plan until it has gathered necessary temporal variables (days remaining, available hours, target subjects) from the student.

### **Sophisticated Agent Workflows**
- **Sequential Chaining**: For mixed-intent queries (e.g., "Check my answer and then explain the concept"), agents are executed sequentially. The **output of the first agent is injected as context** into the second agent, ensuring a seamless, non-fragmented conversation.
- **Shared Memory**: All agents have access to a filtered 10-message sliding window of chat history to maintain semantic continuity.

## 2. High-Precision RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)
To eliminate hallucinations and ensure 100% curriculum alignment, Academic Pulse utilizes a robust RAG pipeline (`rag.ts` & `gemini.ts`).

### **Ingestion & Chunking**
- **Sliding-Window Algorithm**: Raw NCERT PDFs are parsed into 500-character chunks with a **100-character semantic overlap**. This prevents "concept-clipping" at page or paragraph breaks.
- **Vector Storage**: Use of **Supabase pgvector** for storing high-dimensional embeddings generated by `text-embedding-004`.

### **Retrieval & Grounding**
- **Hybrid Similarity Search**: The system performs semantic searches to find top-K relevant chunks. 
- **Client-Side Resilience**: If network latency occurs, the platform switches to a **Local Similarity Fallback**, computing dot-product comparisons on cached arrays to ensure zero-downtime retrieval.
- **Strict Grounding Protocol**: The LLM is wrapped in a "Refusal Boundary"—if the retrieved context does not contain the answer, the AI is programmed to politely decline rather than use general training data.

### **Interactive Asset Injection**
The RAG pipeline doesn't just retrieve text; it retrieves **visual metadata**. 
- **Figure Tokens**: Chunks are tagged with `[FIGURE:X.X]` macros.
- **Frontend Interception**: When the LLM outputs a figure token, the client-side renderer intercepts it and injects a high-quality interactive image, chart, or chemical equation from the platform's asset registry.

## 3. Multimodal Audio Overview (Podcast Engine)
One of the platform's most sophisticated features is the **Thematic Podcast Engine** (`audioOverview.ts`), which transforms dense chapters into studio-quality audio lessons.

### **Generative Scripting**
Students can choose from multiple **Persona Themes**:
- **Story**: Weaves concepts into a narrative with characters.
- **Dramatic**: High-stakes, intense discovery-based narration.
- **Fun**: Playful, energetic, and analogy-heavy.
- **Revision**: High-density, rapid-fire factual review.

### **Multimodal Pipeline**
1.  **Context Assembly**: RAG isolates the most relevant content for a specific topic or full chapter.
2.  **Thematic Generation**: LLM generates a script optimized for speech (no symbols, no markdown).
3.  **Direct TTS Streaming**: Uses `gemini-2.5-flash-preview-tts` to stream audio components.
4.  **WAV Reconstruction**: A custom client-side pipeline decodes base64 PCM data and assembles a valid **44-byte RIFF/WAVE header** directly in the browser, creating a playable Blob URL without server-side processing.

## 4. Multimodal Board Paper System
The **Board Paper Generation & Evaluation** workflow (`boardPapers.ts`) provides a full-cycle simulation of the CBSE examination environment.

### **Generative Complexity Vectors**
Users can generate papers with varied difficulty (Easy, Medium, Hard, Mixed). The AI uses a **Section-Wise Blueprint** to distribute marks across:
- Section A (MCQs)
- Section E (Case-Based with Passages)
- Sections B/C/D (Subjective with OR alternatives)

### **Vision-Powered Evaluation**
Academic Pulse implements a complex multimodal evaluation loop:
1.  **Optical Input**: Students upload photos or PDFs of their handwritten answer sheets.
2.  **Vision Analysis**: Gemini's **Multimodal Vision** capability analyzes the image against the generated paper's JSON schema and marking rubric.
3.  **Granular Analytics**: Instead of just a total score, the system generates a **Performance Matrix**:
    - **Topic-Wise Efficiency**: Identifying "strong," "average," and "weak" areas.
    - **Section-Wise Analysis**: Tracking performance across MCQ vs. Subjective questions.
    - **Feedback Mapping**: Question-by-question justification for marks awarded.

## 5. Procedural Games Hub
The platform converts learning into play through a **Real-Time Game Engine** (`games.ts`).

- **Procedural Generation**: Unlike static quiz apps, games are generated on-the-fly from RAG context.
- **Diverse Mechanics**:
    - **Memory Sprint**: Immediate recall testing.
    - **Case Cracker**: High-level application of concepts to realistic scenarios.
    - **60-Second Challenge**: Pressure-based rapid evaluation.
    - **Build the Answer**: Algorithmic step-sequencing (Drag & Drop) to teach logical answer structuring.
- **Gamified State Management**: "Pulse Points" are calculated based on accuracy, speed, and streaks, then persisted to the user's historical profile to drive engagement.

## 6. Live Study Sessions (LiveKit Integration)
Academic Pulse elevates the learning experience from text-based interaction to real-time conversational engagement through the **Live Study Session** module.

### **Real-time Voice & Interaction**
By clicking the "Live Session" trigger, users enter a high-concurrency, low-latency conversational environment powered by **LiveKit**. This shifts the pedagogical model from asynchronous chat to a synchronous, AI-driven study partner.
- **Conversational AI Agent**: A specialized LiveKit-integrated agent that processes voice input and responds in real-time, simulating a face-to-face tutoring session.
- **Dynamic Session Modes**: Users can either ask specific ad-hoc questions or request a "Full Study Session" where the AI leads a structured lesson through a specific chapter or topic.

### **Sophisticated Real-time Workflows**
- **Low-Latency Synthesis**: The system optimizes the agent's turn-taking and response latency to ensure a natural, fluid conversation without the "robotic pauses" typical of older TTS systems.
- **State Synchronicity**: The Live Session agent is grounded in the same RAG context as the text-based Mentor, ensuring that the voice-based tutor remains strictly within the bounds of the CBSE curriculum.

## 7. Experiential Learning Lab
The **Experiential Learning Lab** (`experientialLearning.ts`) provides a high-fidelity virtual laboratory where students move from abstract theory to hands-on experimental discovery.

### **Interactive Virtual Labs**
Instead of static diagrams, students participate in curriculum-aligned simulations that visualize complex scientific properties. The initial module focuses on **Chapter 3: Metals and Non-Metals**, utilizing a multi-phase discovery workflow:

1.  **Phase 1: Physical Property Simulation (Ductility)**:
    - **Mechanical Interaction**: Students use a "Virtual Grip" system to physically drag and stretch a copper cylinder.
    - **Dynamic State Management**: The system calculates a real-time **Stretch Ratio**, dynamically thinning the cylinder's height and widening its length to simulate the drawing of a wire.
    - **Visual Progression**: The transition from a "block" to a "wire" provides immediate sensory reinforcement of the concept of ductility.

2.  **Phase 2: Functional System Engineering (Conductivity)**:
    - **Component-Based Logic**: Using a drag-and-drop interface, students must correctly place a battery, light bulb, and switch onto a circuit board.
    - **Interactive Wiring Engine**: An SVG-based wiring module allows students to click and drag connections between component ports, simulating real-world circuit design.
    - **Circuit Logic & Animation**: Toggling the virtual switch triggers a **Live Flow Animation**—using SVG `animateMotion` to visualize electron flow—and dynamically updates the bulb's glow filters (`feGaussianBlur`) when the circuit is successfully closed.

### **Pedagogical Reinforcement**
Upon successful completion of the experiment, the platform injects **Knowledge Cards** that bridge the gap between simulation and examination:
- **Curriculum Mapping**: Links the experiment's results to internal RAG facts (e.g., specific conductivity values of silver vs. copper).
- **Aesthetic Immersion**: Uses "Glassmorphism" UI and smooth transitions to maintain high student engagement during complex scientific tasks.
